Coding

Why do I like it?

I really enjoy making things work and seeing how they work. Since I was in elementray school, I would commonly get in slight trouble at school and at home from wanting to take things apart and put them back together again. One time, even, at an engineering field trip, I accidentally shortcircuited an air pressure display because there weren't signs that said "do not touch" and I was very curious to see how the air pressure fluxuated with whatever combinations of switches were pressed...it worked, so I was satisfied. Coding reminds me of that, sometimes. Regardless, my interest in seeing how things work extends to computers. Being dropped into a world where computers just ARE and they just WORK has always left me perplexed. WHY do they work??? HOW do they work???

Personal History:

My history with coding goes back to middle school when I grew a great interest i creating video games. As I saw that landscape widen and become oversaturated, I fell off of my interest in gaame development, but my experience with it, a few years later in high school, left me interested to use it for more practical usages in the real world. From that moment, I had my mind set on what I wanted to do in my life.

What do you want to do with it?

I want to be of use to this world, which relies on computers. I want to help the less knowledgable people, when it comes to computers, understand how the world is working. I want to get to the point where I become the teacher more than the student. This field is very important and more people should treat it as such.
